# Article Quality Checklist

Use this checklist before publishing or merging an article.

## 1. Search Intent

- [ ] The article targets a clear search intent.
- [ ] The title matches the reader's intent.
- [ ] The article answers a concrete question or problem.
- [ ] The target reader is clear.

## 2. Article Structure

- [ ] The article starts with a clear conclusion.
- [ ] The structure is easy to scan.
- [ ] Headings are specific and useful.
- [ ] The article includes a summary.
- [ ] The article includes related articles when possible.

## 3. Practical Value

- [ ] The article includes practical examples or concrete guidance.
- [ ] Beginner articles explain terminology carefully.
- [ ] Beginner articles use tables, diagrams, Mermaid diagrams, or other visual aids when useful.
- [ ] Error-resolution articles include investigation methods.
- [ ] Error-resolution articles include solution steps.
- [ ] Error-resolution articles include command examples when appropriate.
- [ ] The article explains how to confirm that the issue or task is complete.

## 4. AI Product Relevance

- [ ] The article is relevant to AI application development or AI product development.
- [ ] The article connects the topic to implementation, operation, or product decisions.
- [ ] The article avoids vague or generic AI commentary.

## 5. Internal Links

- [ ] The article links to related articles when possible.
- [ ] The article links to relevant tools when possible.
- [ ] Internal links are natural and useful.
- [ ] Links are not forced only for SEO.

## 6. Frontmatter

- [ ] `title` is present.
- [ ] `description` is present.
- [ ] `publishedAt` is present.
- [ ] `updatedAt` is present.
- [ ] `category` is present.
- [ ] `tags` are present.
- [ ] The article uses one primary category.
- [ ] The article uses 3 to 5 tags when possible.
- [ ] `needs_fact_check: true` is set when time-sensitive facts require review.

## 7. Duplication Check

- [ ] Existing published articles were checked.
- [ ] Existing draft articles were checked.
- [ ] The topic is not substantially duplicated.
- [ ] If the topic overlaps, the article has a clearly different angle.

## 8. Style

- [ ] The writing is clear and practical.
- [ ] The article is developer-friendly.
- [ ] The article is accessible to beginners when it targets beginners.
- [ ] The article is not exaggerated.
- [ ] The article is not overly promotional.
- [ ] The article avoids low-quality filler content.

## 9. Technical Check

- [ ] Markdown syntax is valid.
- [ ] Internal links use valid paths.
- [ ] Code blocks are formatted correctly.
- [ ] Command examples are realistic.
- [ ] `npm run build` succeeds when publishing into the web app.
